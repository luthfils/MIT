About

Bio Farma

The Pharmaceutical BUMN Holding was formed on January 31, 2020, consisting of PT Bio Farma (Persero), PT Kimia Farma Tbk, and PT Indofarma Tbk, as well as PT INUKI which only joined in 2021 with the parent company, PT Bio Farma (Persero). Members of BUMN Pharmaceutical Holding have different business focuses, namely PT Bio Farma (Persero) which focuses on the life science business, PT Kimia Farma Tbk which focuses on the drug business and PT Indofarma Tbk which focuses on the herbal and medical device business, and PT INUKI which operates in the nuclear technology industry. The purpose of the establishment of the Pharmaceutical SOE Holding is to strengthen the independence of the national pharmaceutical industry, increase product availability and create joint innovations in the supply of pharmaceutical products.

MIT Hacking Medicine

MIT Hacking Medicine has facilitated nearly across a dozen countries and multiple U.S. states. It aims to energizing the healthcare community and accelerating medical innovation.

MIT Hacking Medicine routinely collaborates with prominent organizations and corporations. Examples include The Kauffman Foundation, Massachusetts General Hospital, Emergency Nurses Association, Pfizer, Microsoft, Samsung, GE, Merck, and AthenaHealth, to name a few. The group has also been featured in avenues such as SXSW, Wall Street Journal, Slate and Wired.

Hackathon Track

Up Stream

Pharmaceutical & Biotechnology

Bio Farma group (incl. Kimia Farma & Indofarma) has 74 million USD overstock of products including vaccines, drugs, and medical devices. In spite of overstock, the customer does not always find the product they want available. Its current supply chain data is not streamed properly, nor calculated uniformly, making it difficult to find the cause of the problem. A domino effect could occur in any chain, causing the collapse of all downstream chains and result in the unavailability of product.

Down Stream

Healthcare Providers & Retailers

In Indonesia’s e-retailing landscape, no healthcare player has offered an end to end, comprehensive offering. There are still gaps in the players’ business model and considerable room for improvement to optimize sales in existing outlets: Supply chain, packaging, patient’s online-to-offline experience. Most of the players also do not address continuity of care pain points. Likewise, Kimia Farma Mobile’s current strength in e-pharmacy retail has not addressed the need for continuity of care nor strengthened its online-to-offline experience.
